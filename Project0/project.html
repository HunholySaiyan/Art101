Animal Affected Pollution

Air pollution is the result of the release of contaminants into the air through many forms of emissions. These contaminants can prove harmful to the health of both human and animal life because they linger in the air that all living things must breathe in order to survive. The most common source of emitting such pollutants is from the burning of fossil fuels, which we depend on for generating energy. Fossil fuels release harmful chemicals into the atmosphere, which can affect the health of many. Another form of contaminant can be carbon dioxide, which is the main contributor to climate change since it raises the Earthâ€™s temperature. Smog is also formed when these contaminants are mixed with sunlight. Factories and industries would also release harmful chemicals into the atmosphere, such as carbon monoxide. In farms, pesticides and insecticides are used to keep crops safe from pests, which in turn pollute the air with the chemicals used in them. Air contaminants can also come naturally. For example, smoke is also emitted from volcanic eruptions and natural forest fires. Aside from the health issues, air pollution contributes to global warming and the destruction of the planet's ozone layer, which helps keep life safe from the Sun's dangerous rays.
